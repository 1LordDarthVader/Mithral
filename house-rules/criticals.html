Criticals

Critical Hits

Adamantine uses the "double the dice" method of critical hits. This means you double the number of damage dice rolled and add modifiers once. For example, if you are using a nonmagical longsword and have a Strength modifier of +5, a critical hit will deal 2d8+5 damage.

Dice added through temporary features, such as Divine Smite or Hunter's Mark, are also doubled by this.

Critical Saving Throws

If a creature is subjected to a saving throw that allows it to take half damage on a successful save, special occurrences happen on critical rolls.

A natural 20 will result in the creature taking no damage (as though they had the Evasion feature). A natural 1 will result in the creature taking double the dice of damage.

Criticals and Disadvantage

Rolling a natural 20 when you have disadvantage will allow you to keep the natural 20, though you lose the critical effects of the natural 20.

Critical Cards

When a player rolls a natural 20 on an attack roll or saving throw or the DM rolls a natural 1 on an attack roll or saving throw against that player, they gain a critical card. These cards vary in effect, and the effect will be listed on the card.

When a player rolls a natural 1 on an attack roll or saving throw or the DM rolls a natural 20, the DM gains a critical card. These work identically to the player's version, though the Fortuitous Circumstance card is useless for the DM.

The DM can also award critical cards as awards, similar to Inspiration.

Player or DM, each person may only have a maximum of 3 critical cards at any given time. If you are to gain a fourth card, you must discard one of the four.
