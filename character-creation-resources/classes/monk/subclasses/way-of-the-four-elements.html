Way of the Four Elements

You follow a monastic tradition that teaches you to harness the elements. When you focus your ki, you can align yourself with the forces of creation and bend the four elements to your will, using them as an extension of your body. Some members of this tradition dedicate themselves to a single element, but others weave the elements together.

Many monks of this tradition tattoo their bodies with representations of their ki powers, commonly imagined as coiling dragons, but also as phoenixes, fish, plants, mountains, and cresting waves.

Elemental Strikes

Starting at 3rd level, your strikes carry elemental energy with them. When you deal damage to a target with an unarmed strike, you can change the damage type to acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder.

Cantrips

You learn two cantrips of your choice from the /elemental-discipline-list">elemental discipline list. You learn an additional cantrip of your choice at 10th level.

Casting Spells With Ki

In order to cast spells, you must expend a number of ki points equal to the level of spell being cast.

Spells Known of 1st-Level and Higher

You know two 1st-level elemental disciplines of your choice.

The Spells Known column of the Four Elements Spellcasting table shows when you learn more elemental disciplines of 1st level or higher.

Whenever you gain a level in this class, you can replace one of the elemental disciplines you know with another discipline of your choice from the elemental discipline list.

Spellcasting Ability

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your elemental disciplines, since your ability to cast spells comes from your ki. Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Spell Striker

Starting at 7th level, when you use your action to cast a cantrip, you can use your Flurry of Blows feature.

Elemental Fury

Starting at 11th level, your elemental magic tears through defenses easily. You ignore resistance to acid, cold, fire, lightning, and thunder damage.

Elemental Body

Starting at 17th level, you can take on a form of pure elemental power. You can expend 9 ki points to cast the Form of the Elements spell.
