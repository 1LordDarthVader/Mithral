Flame Heart Archetype

The flame heart is one of the elemental cousins of the storm heart. They are both known as half-sparks, individuals extremely talented in one aspect of magic. As their name suggests, a flame hearts talent is fire.

Like the Storm heart, their talents mainly emerge from a magical phenomenon. The flame heart's body can absorb massive amounts of heat, and generate just as much on its own. This often caused by emotional duress, or stressful situation in their early years, but as they gain experience they learn to control it, using it as both a tool and a weapon to rival the pyromancer's claim of mastery over the fire.

Living Inferno

At 3rd level, when you choose this archetype, your talent with your flame allows you to reap a path of charred destruction. As an action, you can cause each creature within 15 feet of you to make a Dexterity saving throw equal to 8 + your Charisma modifier + your proficiency bonus or take 3d6 fire damage or half as much on a successful save.

The damage of this ability by 1d6 at 7th level, and again at 10th, 13th, 16th and 19th level.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Bright Warrior

Starting at 7th level, your Living Inferno feature ignores resistance to fire damage and you gain resistance to fire damage.

Additionally, you are no longer affected by extreme heat or extreme cold environments, and you can choose to shed bright light up to 30 feet around you, with an additional 20 feet of dim light beyond.

Flame Eater

Starting at 10th level, fire becomes a viable food source of food to you. You can consume a flame as small as a campfire by breathing in the flames from 5 feet away to gain the benefits of a full meal.

Additionally, if you are forced make a Dexterity saving throw to avoid fire damage from a spell or ability such as fireball, you can use your reaction to consume the erupting flames and cause the spell or ability to deal half damage to you, and any other creature who had to make the save. You regain one use of your Living Inferno feature if you take 15 or more fire damage after using this feature.

Hellish Force

Starting at 15th level, the intensity of you can knock enemies to ground You can cause creatures who fail their Dexterity saving throw against your Living Inferno feature to be pushed back 10 feet and knocked prone.

Lord of the Flames

Starting at 18th level, you can expend a use of your Action Surge to cast the Firestorm spell. However, the first 10 foot cube must be placed adjacent to you. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

Once you do this you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

Flame Heart Exploits

Half-spark's Knowledge

Whenever you make an Intelligence (Arcana) or Intelligence (Nature) check, you can expend an Exploit Die and add it to your roll. You can use this Exploit after you roll, but before you know whether you succeed or fail.

Flame Strike

When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend an Exploit Die to deal additional fire damage to the target, equal to your Exploit Die + your Charisma modifier.

Flaming Boost

As a bonus action, you can expend an Exploit Die to launch yourself through the air in a fiery burst. You gain a flying speed equal to your walking speed until the end of your current turn. If you remain in the air when your turn ends, you fall.
