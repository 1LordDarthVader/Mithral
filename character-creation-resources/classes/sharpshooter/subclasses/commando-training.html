Commando Training

Commandos are the most advanced special forces units in any army. Often seen as a one-man army, they are masters of infiltration, assassination, and suceeding where others cannot with superior firepower and advanced training for almost any situation.

Elite Fitness

Starting at 3rd level, your hit point maximum increases by 3 points, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a sharpshooter level. In addition, you gain proficiency with heavy armor and you ignore the Strength requirement of heavy armor.

Hand-to-Hand Training

Also at 3rd level, You have been extensively trained in hand-to-hand combat, advancing your melee strikes. You gain the following benefits:
  • You gain proficiency in the Athletics skill.
  • Your unarmed strike deals 1d6 bludgeoning damage instead of the normal damage for an unarmed strike.
  • You can attempt to grapple a creature within 5 feet of you as a bonus action.
  • When you make an unarmed strike with advantage, or if there is a creature friendly to you within 5 feet of the target, you can add 1d8 bludgeoning damage. This bonus damage increases to 2d8 at 7th level, 3d8 at 10th, 4d8 at 15th, and 5d8 at 18th level.

Hearty Physique

Starting at 7th level, you can use your Constitution modifier in place of your Strength modifier for ability checks and saving throws.

Master of the Shadows

Starting at 10th level, when in dim light or darkness you have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks and unarmed strikes.

In addition, when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike and the target is surprised, the attack is considered a critical hit.

Determined

Starting at 15th level, when you are dropped to 0 hit points, you can instead drop to a number of hit points equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum of 1).

Once you use this feature you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest.

Hail of Shots

Starting at 18th level, you can fire a rapid stream of ranged attacks. Using your action and bonus action, you can make a number of attacks with a ranged weapon you are holding equal to your proficiency bonus.

You can use this feature once, and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.
